// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your product images for crushed rock, washed rock, and AASHTO-grade storm gallery stone are almost certainly sitting on your server with zero alt-text, no JSON-LD LocalBusiness or Product schema, and factory-default EXIF data that tells Google nothing about Johnson Creek Quarry or your Roy pit location. Right now, a civil contractor in Lacey typing "WSDOT approved AASHTO storm gallery stone Tenino" is landing on a competitor's page because your images carry no structured signal confirming you are that source. Every untagged photo of your crushed product line is an invisible asset in a market where structured image data directly influences local pack placement.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your public works contractors and site superintendents coordinating WSDOT-spec material procurement are leaving without a purchase order because you are asking them to trust a text claim with zero visual corroboration. You state WSDOT approval and AASHTO compliance, but there is not a single photo of a scale ticket, a certified gradation report, a loaded belly-dump, or a stockpile with a legible product sign anywhere visible on the site. In spec-driven procurement, unsupported compliance claims read as liability, not credibility.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Any regional aggregate supplier running geo-tagged jobsite delivery photos, timestamped scale house imagery, and product-specific stockpile shots with visible gradation signage is visually closing the contract before your sales line even rings. A contractor who has seen a competitor's documented delivery sequence — truck, scale ticket, placed material — has already built operational confidence that your three phone numbers and a street address cannot manufacture. That visual gap costs you repeat commercial accounts, which in bulk aggregate is where margin actually lives.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a single structured shoot across all three locations — Johnson Creek Quarry, Roy pit, and the 167 Puyallup stockpile site — capturing product stockpiles with legible grade signage, active scale house operations, loaded fleet trucks bearing your branding, and the Puyallup Class 1 soil dump in operation. Every image gets embedded with location-specific EXIF coordinates, descriptive filenames tied to product and location (e.g., `wsdot-aashto-storm-gallery-stone-johnson-creek-tenino-wa.jpg`), and alt-text that mirrors the exact search language your buyers use at bid time. Pair that with JSON-LD Product and LocalBusiness schema deployed site-wide and you convert a static brochure site into a structured data asset that works every hour your scale house is closed.
